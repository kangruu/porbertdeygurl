01

February — The Goodbye

February was the month everything became quiet.

For a while, I kept looking back at conversations, wondering if there was something I missed, something I could have said differently.

But some endings are not meant to be solved.
They are meant to be accepted.

At first, I thought the hardest part was losing you.
Later, I realized the harder part was being given another chance and not knowing if taking it would heal what was broken or simply reopen old wounds.

There was a time when I wanted nothing more than for us to find our way back to each other.
And when that possibility finally appeared, I found myself carrying memories I had never truly learned how to put down.

I still cared.
I still wanted you.
But love alone could not erase the hurt we had both experienced.

So in the end, I made a choice that broke my own heart.

Not because I stopped loving you, but because I was afraid that if we tried again before we had truly healed, we would only relive the same pain and lose each other all over again.

And so February became the month I learned that sometimes letting go is not the absence of love.

Sometimes, it is love taking a different form.

The kind that chooses someone's peace even when it means living with their absence.

02

March — Learning to Carry the Silence

March felt strange.

The habit of sharing stories, random thoughts, and little moments was still there.

Many times I wanted to send a message.
Many times I stopped myself.

Not because I stopped caring,
but because I was learning that care can exist without interruption.

I learned that not every feeling needs to be delivered.
Some are simply carried.

There were moments when something good happened and you were the first person I thought of telling.
There were moments when life felt heavy, and I found myself looking for a conversation that no longer belonged to my days.

The absence was noticeable.
Not loud enough to stop life from moving forward, but quiet enough to be felt.

Some days I wondered how you were doing.
Whether you were happy.
Whether the things you hoped for were slowly finding their way to you.

I never stopped caring.

I just stopped reaching.

And somewhere between wanting to message you and choosing not to, I began to understand that missing someone and respecting the distance they need can exist at the same time.

March was not the month I moved on.

It was the month I learned how to carry your absence without trying to fill it.

03

April — Becoming

April was not about forgetting.

It was about becoming.

Little by little I returned to my studies, my goals, and the version of myself that still had dreams waiting to be pursued.

There were still moments when memories appeared unexpectedly.

But they no longer felt like wounds.

They felt like reminders that something beautiful once existed.

Still, I missed you.

Not every day in the same way, and not with the same heaviness as before, but in the quiet moments between everything else.

Sometimes a song, a thought, or a simple moment would bring you back to mind.

And for a while, I would find myself smiling at a memory before continuing with my day.

I learned that healing and missing someone can exist together.

That moving forward does not always mean leaving everything behind.

Some people remain part of us long after they are gone.

April was not the month I forgot you.

It was the month I learned how to grow while carrying the memories with me.

04

May — Gratitude

By May, I found myself smiling at memories instead of running from them.

Not every story lasts forever.

But that does not make it meaningless.

You taught me things I would never have learned alone.

Patience.
Understanding.
Courage.

And for that, I will always be grateful.

For a long time, I thought the value of a story was measured by how long it lasted.

Now I think differently.

Some people stay for years and leave little behind.
Others become part of your life for a moment and change the way you see the world forever.

You were one of those people for me.

The memories no longer felt heavy.
They felt warm.

Like opening an old book and finding pages that still make you smile.

Of course, there were moments when I still missed you.

But by then, the feeling had changed.

It was no longer about wishing things had been different.

It was about being thankful that they happened at all.

May became the month I stopped asking why things ended and started appreciating that they happened.

And for the first time in a long while, I found peace in that.

05

June — Looking Forward

Maybe someday, it could still be us.

I still find myself hoping for that, in a quiet way I don't always say out loud.

But right now, I don't think I can give you what you truly deserve in life.

Not because I don't care.

But because I still want to grow more as a person, to build myself into someone more stable, more ready, and more certain of who I am.

And I want the same for you too.

I want you to achieve the life you've been dreaming of, without being held back or limited by me in my current state.

So for now, I think it's better that we both focus on our own paths.

On becoming better.

On reaching the goals we truly want for ourselves.

I don't want us to love each other in a way that stops us from growing.

I want us to become the kind of people who are strong on our own, not because we don't care, but because we respect what we are trying to build.

If life ever brings us back together, I want it to be when we are both ready—not when we are still figuring out how to hold ourselves together.

And if it doesn't bring us back, I still want you to know that I will always be grateful for you.

For what we had.

For what we learned.

For the version of me that existed because you were part of my life.

So I will let you go for now, not as something forgotten, but as something I respect enough to release.

And maybe someday, when the timing is right and we've both grown into the lives we're working toward… it could still be us.

But for now, I'll quietly root for your happiness from where I am.

June feels different.

Not because everything has healed.

But because I have finally stopped measuring my days by what was lost.

Life keeps moving.
Dreams keep growing.

And while part of me still wonders about "us," I've learned that timing matters just as much as feeling.

Maybe I wanted to go back.

Maybe I still do, in some quiet parts of me.

But I also know that going back without being fully ready would only risk repeating the same things we once tried to move past.

So instead of holding on to what could have been, I choose to focus on what I need to become.

I want my self, and maybe your self too, to be more focused on our goals in life.

On what we truly want to achieve for ourselves right now.

Not out of distance or forgetting, but out of respect for the lives we are still building.

And if someday our paths meet again in a better time, I hope we can look at each other not as people trying to fix the past, but as people who grew through it.

But if not, I will still be grateful that you were part of my story.

Because even if we don't end up as "us," you will always be part of the reason I learned how to grow.

07

Afterwards — The Things You Made

I didn't know about it at first.

The video you made.
The portrait of me.
And the LEGO you bought.

I only found out later… and it honestly made me pause for a moment and just smile.

Because it felt like discovering little pieces of you I didn't get to see while everything was still happening between us.

You were creating things with me in them, quietly, naturally… like I was still part of your thoughts in those moments.

And somehow, that stayed with me in a soft way.

The portrait especially… I keep going back to it in my mind.

There's something about knowing someone actually sat down and chose to draw you. Not just see you, but spend time turning that into something real. It feels gentle in a way I can't really explain.

And the LEGO… that one made me happy.

It's such a simple thing, but it felt personal. Like you saw something small in me and thought, "this is something he'd like," and decided to give it. That kind of thoughtfulness is rare in a way that makes you quietly appreciate it more when you realize it.

And there's another video too… the birthday video you made for me.

Whenever I miss you, I somehow find myself going back to that video.

It gives me this warm feeling I can't really put into words… like I'm closer to you for a moment, even if it's just through a screen.

I really do miss you. A lot more than I probably ever say directly.

I think what made me smile the most is realizing I was still in your world in those moments, and I was fully aware of it.

Still being thought of.
Still being remembered in small, creative ways.

It makes everything feel a little warmer when I think about it now.

So I just want to say thank you.

For the portrait you made.
For the LEGO you chose.
For the birthday video you made for me.
For the way you kept me in your thoughts in your own quiet, beautiful way.

It makes me happy to know that there were moments like that between us that I only got to discover later, but still get to feel now.

And maybe that's my favorite part about it—

that even after everything, I can still find something that makes me smile when I think of you.

Some people stay forever.

Some become beautiful memories.

Both are gifts.

— A letter I never sent,
but always meant to write.

— The End —
